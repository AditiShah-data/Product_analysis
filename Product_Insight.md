# Key Insights

1. November 2019 recorded the highest customer engagement, leading in product views, add-to-cart actions, cart removals, and purchases. Most interactions involved lower-priced products.

2. Premium products attracted greater attention in December 2019 and January 2020, but this interest did not translate into proportional purchases, indicating lower conversion for expensive items.

3. Cart removals were highest for high-priced products during December and January, suggesting price sensitivity before checkout.

4. December generated fewer purchases but higher purchase value, showing that customers who bought during this period preferred premium products.

5. Afternoon was the peak activity period across all user actions (views, add-to-cart, purchases, and cart removals), making it the most important engagement window.

# Recommendations

1. Increase promotion of affordable products, as they consistently generated higher engagement and purchase volumes.

2. Offer discounts, coupons, or EMI options on premium products to reduce cart abandonment and improve conversion rates.

3. Launch targeted remarketing campaigns for users who remove expensive products from their carts, encouraging them to complete purchases.

4. Schedule marketing campaigns and promotional notifications during afternoon hours to maximize customer reach and engagement.

5. Create a balanced product strategy combining affordable and premium products, leveraging high traffic from low-cost items while maintaining revenue from high-value purchases.
